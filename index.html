I’m an experienced population geographer, migration expert, passionate communicator, policy advisor, creative thinker, and inspiring leader. I’m currently based in Potsdam and Wiesbaden, Germany.

With an academic background in migration and demography, I enjoy perceiving the world in new ways and looking beyond the obvious to solve problems and generate new ideas. In my academic career, I combined scientific research with a passion for data analytics, visualization and communication, providing evidence-based policy advice and helping inform the public about demographic change and migration.

I grew up in Germany, studied geography in Regensburg and Tuebingen, and completed my PhD in geography at the University of Queensland, Australia, in 2010. I then moved to Austria, working at the Vienna Institute of Demography on international migration statistics and population projections until 2016. My next stop was the Netherlands, where I served as Assistant Professor in Population Geography at the University of Groningen. In 2017, I returned to Germany to become Research Director at the Federal Institute for Population Research (BiB).

From October 2024 to March 2025, I took on a new challenge as Head of Department 22 "Non-University Research" at the Ministry of Science, Research, and Culture of the State of Brandenburg. After 18 years in academia, this role offered a great opportunity to transition into science policy and to support non-university research institutes in Brandenburg. In April 2025, I chose to temporarily return to the BiB in Wiesbaden. I'm currently open to new roles and excited for new adventures and opportunities to grow, collaborate, and create impact.
